Research

By Kirsten Gotting 01 January 0001

Current

I am broadly interested in evolutionary genomics, and for my PhD work I have joined Cameron Currie’s lab at the University of Wisconsin - Madison. In the Currie lab, we study how symbiosis shapes biodiversity. Currently, I am working with the fungus farming ant symbiosis to understand the selective forces that shape pathogen resistance in the specialized mycoparasite Escovopsis.

Previous

Before joining my PhD program at the UW-Madison I worked as a bioinformaticist in Alejandro Sánchez’s lab at The Stowers Institute for Medical Research. I worked on a variety of projects in developmental biology using many different animals, with our primary research interests residing in understanding what rules govern regeneration and how regeneration differs from primary development. I contributed bioinformatics analyses to the Schmidtea mediterranea genome browser and the associated publication. Additionally I contributed analyses to publications ranging from egf signaling during neoblast repopulation, the embryonic origins of adult stem cells, and how a nuclear hormone receptor is required for reproductive organ regeneration.

I also had the oportunity to collaborate with the Swalla lab at the University of Washington and contributed bioinformatic analyses in understanding head regeneration in the hemichordate Ptychodera flava.
